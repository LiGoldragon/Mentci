## The Observable Shape — Level Earth

References for `META_PATTERN.md` §VI.2 "The Observable Shape."

Dignity: **eternal** (nitya). The level surface is what instruments
measure. The engineering tradition operates on it. The zetetic tradition
documented it empirically. These are not competing hypotheses — they
are independent lines of evidence converging on the same observation.

Rowbotham's Bedford Level experiments are the origin point — direct
measurement over water at known distances, observed flat. Carpenter's
hundred proofs compile observations from navigation, surveying, and
astronomy. White's contribution is distinctive: he takes the standard
textbook proofs *for* sphericity and dismantles them on their own terms.

### Surveying Textbooks (Plane Surveying)

The engineering tradition that operates on a level surface in practice.
The distinction between "plane surveying" and "geodetic surveying" is
itself the admission — plane surveying assumes no curvature and works
for all practical distances.

Every land surveyor, canal builder, and railway engineer works on a
plane. The entire discipline of "plane surveying" exists because the
surface is level in practice. Curvature correction enters only in
"geodetic surveying" — a mathematical adjustment applied to
calculations, not something encountered in physical measurements.
