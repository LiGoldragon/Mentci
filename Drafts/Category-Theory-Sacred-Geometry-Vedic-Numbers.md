## 1. Category Theory and the Number Chain 2-3-7-12-36-72-360

### The Number 2: The Walking Arrow

The number 2 is the simplest non-trivial category in the most literal sense. The **walking arrow** (also called the **interval category** or **directed interval**) is the category **2** = {0 -> 1}, consisting of exactly two objects and one non-identity morphism between them. This is formally "the ordinal number 2 regarded as a poset, regarded as a category" (nLab). It is the fundamental building block of category theory:

- Natural transformations between functors F, G: C -> D correspond to functors C x **2** -> D.
- The walking arrow generates the notion of **homotopy** in categorical settings.
- The opposite category **2**^op = {1 -> 0} is isomorphic to **2** itself -- making 2 the simplest self-dual non-trivial ordinal category.
- Categorically, 2 encodes the most primitive distinction: source vs. target, domain vs. codomain, true vs. false (the subobject classifier in **Set** is {0, 1}).

### The Number 3: Triangulated Categories and the Simplex Category

The number 3 is the engine of **triangulation** in category theory.

**The Simplex Category Delta.** Objects are finite non-empty ordinals [n] = {0, 1, ..., n}. The object [2] = {0 -> 1 -> 2} is the **2-simplex** (triangle), and it is from this that all simplicial structure radiates. The simplex category is generated by:
- **Face maps** delta_i: [n-1] -> [n] (injections skipping element i)
- **Degeneracy maps** sigma_i: [n+1] -> [n] (surjections doubling element i)

These satisfy the **simplicial identities**, which are the combinatorial spine of homological algebra. Simplicial sets (presheaves on Delta) are one of the fundamental models for homotopy types.

**Triangulated Categories.** Defined by Verdier (1963, thesis under Grothendieck), a triangulated category is an additive category with a shift functor T and a class of **distinguished triangles** X -> Y -> Z -> TX. The "triangle" has three vertices and three morphisms -- the number 3 is intrinsic. The axioms TR0-TR5 encode composition of triangles, with the **octahedral axiom** (TR5) governing how three composable morphisms generate a coherent system of triangles.

**The (2,3,7) triangle group.** The hyperbolic triangle with angles pi/2, pi/3, pi/7 has the remarkable property that 1 - 1/2 - 1/3 - 1/7 = 1/42 is the smallest positive value achievable by 1 - 1/p - 1/q - 1/r for integers p,q,r >= 2. This yields Hurwitz's theorem: a genus-g Riemann surface has at most 84(g-1) automorphisms. The (2,3,7) triple thus represents a universal extremal structure built from the first three numbers of the chain.

### The Number 7: Generators and the Fano Plane

The number 7 governs the **Fano plane** PG(2,2), the smallest projective plane:
- 7 points, 7 lines, 3 points per line, 3 lines per point.
- Automorphism group = **PSL(2,7)**, order 168 = 7 x 24 = 7 x 8 x 3.
- PSL(2,7) is the second-smallest non-abelian simple group (after A_5 of order 60).
- PSL(2,7) is a **Hurwitz group** -- the automorphism group of the Klein quartic, the genus-3 surface achieving the 84(g-1) = 168 bound.

**Connection to octonions.** The 7 imaginary units e_1, ..., e_7 of the octonions correspond to the 7 points of the Fano plane. The 7 lines encode the multiplication rules: if e_i, e_j, e_k lie cyclically on a line, then e_i * e_j = e_k. The Fano plane IS the multiplication table of the octonions, compressed into a projective geometry. The nonzero elements of F_2^3 (= Z_2 x Z_2 x Z_2, which has 2^3 - 1 = 7 elements) label both the points and the imaginary units.

**7 classical planets.** The ordering of the days of the week follows the "planetary hours" scheme: Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn -- a cyclic structure on 7 elements. The group Z/7Z acts on this set, and the "star polygon" ordering (connecting every 3rd or 4th element) produces the familiar week-day sequence from the Chaldean order of planetary spheres.

## 2. Sacred Geometry and Category Theory

### The Flower of Life and Hexagonal Lattice

The Flower of Life is constructed iteratively:

The hexagonal lattice A_2 is the root lattice of the Lie algebra sl(3), connecting the Flower of Life directly to the representation theory of SU(3). The lattice's Voronoi cells are regular hexagons, and its symmetry group is the dihedral group D_6 of order 12.

### Platonic Solids as Categories and Duality

Each Platonic solid P defines a category **Cat(P)**:
- **Objects:** vertices of P
- **Morphisms:** paths along edges (with identity = staying at a vertex)
- The **free category** on the directed graph underlying P

**Duality is an exact functor.** The duality operation swaps vertices <-> faces and preserves edges. Categorically, dualizing a polyhedron P to P* creates a contravariant functor between their face posets (ordered by inclusion). The key dualities:

- Tetrahedron is **self-dual**: the face poset of the tetrahedron is isomorphic to its opposite. This is a categorical fixed point of the duality involution.
- Cube <-> Octahedron: the cube's 8 vertices become the octahedron's 8 faces. Both share the same rotation group (S_4, order 24) and the same binary cover (2O, order 48).
- Dodecahedron <-> Icosahedron: the dodecahedron's 20 vertices become the icosahedron's 20 faces. Both share the rotation group A_5 (order 60) and binary cover 2I (order 120).

The ADE classification unifies this: the McKay correspondence maps finite subgroups of SU(2) to extended Dynkin diagrams. The McKay quiver's nodes are irreducible representations of the binary group, and edges encode tensor product decomposition with the fundamental 2D representation. The resulting graph IS the extended Dynkin diagram.

### Projective Duality and the Opposite Category

In projective geometry, **duality** swaps points and lines (in the plane) or points and hyperplanes (in general). Categorically, this is precisely the passage from a category C to its **opposite category C^op**, which reverses all morphisms while preserving objects. The key properties:

- (C^op)^op = C. Duality is an involution.
- A **self-dual** structure is one where C is isomorphic to C^op (the tetrahedron, projective planes with polarity).
- **Projective duality** in PG(n,k) is a contravariant endofunctor that swaps k-dimensional subspaces with (n-1-k)-dimensional subspaces.

The Fano plane PG(2,2) is self-dual: its 7 points and 7 lines are interchangeable under the duality, and its automorphism group PSL(2,7) acts on both.

### The Vesica Piscis as a Categorical Diagram

The vesica piscis -- the intersection of two equal circles whose centers lie on each other's circumference -- has precise mathematical properties:
- The height-to-width ratio is **sqrt(3)** (the vesica contains two equilateral triangles).
- Each arc spans exactly **1/3 of its circle** (120 degrees = 360/3).
- The area = (1/6)(4*pi - 3*sqrt(3)) * r^2.

**Categorical interpretation as a pullback/pushout.** Consider two "domains" (the two circles) as objects A and B in a category, with a common ambient space C. The vesica piscis is the **pullback** (fiber product) A x_C B -- the maximal subobject that maps into both A and B compatibly. Its dual interpretation: starting from the intersection region, the two circles are the **pushout** (amalgamated sum) -- the minimal object into which the intersection maps to recover both circles.

More precisely, the vesica piscis is the **equalizer** of the two inclusion maps of the intersection into the respective circles, and the whole Flower of Life construction is an iterated pushout along vesica piscis inclusions.

### Sri Yantra: Triangulated Structure

The Sri Yantra consists of **9 interlocking triangles** (4 upward-pointing Shiva triangles, 5 downward-pointing Shakti triangles) that create **43 smaller triangles** arranged in 5 concentric rings. The construction is governed by at least **18 triple-concurrency conditions** (sets of three lines meeting at a point), making it a highly constrained geometric configuration.

From a categorical standpoint:
- The 9 base triangles form objects; their intersections form morphisms.
- The 43 resulting regions form a **simplicial-like decomposition** -- not a standard simplicial complex (the overlapping triangles create non-simplicial intersections), but a cellular decomposition with triangulated structure.
- The 4+5 = 9 division mirrors the polarity structure: the duality between upward (masculine/solar) and downward (feminine/lunar) triangles encodes a Z/2Z grading on the triangle set.
- The bindu (central point) serves as a terminal object -- every triangle chain eventually converges there.

## 3. Vedic Mathematics and Number Mysticism

### The Vedic Square

The Vedic square is the 9x9 multiplication table reduced by **digital root** (repeatedly summing digits until a single digit remains). For example, 7 x 8 = 56, digital root = 5 + 6 = 11, digital root = 1 + 1 = 2.

The resulting table reveals:

**Group-theoretic properties:**
- The digital root operation is equivalent to arithmetic modulo 9 (with 9 playing the role of 0). The structure is the multiplicative monoid of **Z/9Z**.
- Row 9 and column 9 are all 9s (the zero element absorbs).
- Row 3 and column 3 cycle through {3, 6, 9} only -- the subgroup 3*Z/9Z = Z/3Z.
- Row 1 is the identity permutation. The rows for digits coprime to 9 ({1,2,4,5,7,8}) are permutations of {1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9}, forming the group **(Z/9Z)*** of order phi(9) = 6.
- The group (Z/9Z)* is isomorphic to **Z/6Z** (cyclic of order 6). Its generators are the elements of order 6: {2, 5} (since 2^6 = 64, digital root 1; and 5^6 = 15625, digital root 1).
- The Vedic square has bilateral symmetry along the main diagonal (commutativity of multiplication) and additional symmetries from the group structure.

## 4. Astrological Structures as Categories

### The Zodiac as Z/12Z

The 12 zodiac signs form the cyclic group **Z/12Z** under the successor operation (Aries -> Taurus -> ... -> Pisces -> Aries). This is a category with:
- **1 object** (the zodiac "wheel")
- **12 morphisms** (rotations by 0, 30, 60, ..., 330 degrees)
- Or equivalently, **12 objects** (the signs) with a unique morphism between any ordered pair (making it a groupoid quotient of the cyclic action).

The subgroup lattice of Z/12Z mirrors astrological aspect structure:
- Z/12Z has subgroups of orders 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12 (all divisors of 12).
- Each subgroup generates a regular polygon inscribed in the zodiac wheel.

### Planetary Dignities as Functors

Each planet P defines a **dignity functor** D_P: **Zodiac** -> **Strength**, where:
- **Zodiac** is the category of 12 signs
- **Strength** is the ordered set {Detriment < Peregrine < Dignity < Exaltation}

The dignity functor assigns to each sign a strength level for planet P. The classical scheme has:
- Each of the 7 planets rules 1-2 signs (domicile).
- Each planet is exalted in exactly 1 sign.
- Detriment = the sign opposite domicile. Exaltation's opposite = fall.

**This is a contravariant structure:** the opposition morphism (multiplication by -1 in Z/12Z) swaps domicile <-> detriment and exaltation <-> fall. So there is a natural transformation between D_P and D_P composed with the opposition involution.

The assignment of rulers to signs can be modeled as a functor **R: Planets -> Signs** (or more precisely, a profunctor, since some planets rule two signs). The Thema Mundi (the mythical "birth chart of the universe") encodes this assignment: Sun rules Leo, Moon rules Cancer, and the remaining 5 planets rule pairs of signs symmetrically about the Cancer-Leo axis.

### The 7 Classical Planets and the Week

The 7 classical planets (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn) generate the 7-day week through the **planetary hours** system:
- Order the planets by decreasing orbital period: Saturn, Jupiter, Mars, Sun, Venus, Mercury, Moon.
- Assign 24 hours cyclically. The planet ruling the first hour of each day names that day.
- Starting from Saturn (Saturday): the sequence of day-rulers is Saturn, Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus -- i.e., skipping every 2 in the Chaldean order.

This is precisely the action of multiplication by 4 (mod 7) on Z/7Z:
- Chaldean order: 0=Saturn, 1=Jupiter, 2=Mars, 3=Sun, 4=Venus, 5=Mercury, 6=Moon
- Day order: 0, 3, 6, 2, 5, 1, 4 -- which is {0, 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18} mod 7 = {0, 3, 6, 2, 5, 1, 4}
- This is multiplication by 3 (or equivalently, by 24 mod 7 = 3).

Since gcd(3, 7) = 1, this generates all of Z/7Z, confirming the 7-day week visits all planets. The multiplicative group (Z/7Z)* has order 6, and 3 is a generator (3^1=3, 3^2=2, 3^3=6, 3^4=4, 3^5=5, 3^6=1 mod 7).

### Houses as a Fibered Category over the Zodiac

The 12 astrological houses form a structure that rotates relative to the zodiac based on the Ascendant (rising sign). This is naturally modeled as a **Grothendieck fibration**:

- **Base category B:** The zodiac Z/12Z (or more precisely, the continuous circle S^1, discretized to 12 signs).
- **Total category E:** The house system, where each "fiber" E_b over a zodiac position b contains the house assignments for that Ascendant.
- **Fibration p: E -> B** projects each house configuration to its Ascendant sign.

For the **equal house system**, the fiber over Ascendant b is simply a translated copy of Z/12Z: the i-th house cusp is at position (b + 30*(i-1)) mod 360. This makes the fibration trivial (a product B x Z/12Z). But for **unequal house systems** (Placidus, Koch, etc.), the fibers depend nontrivially on latitude and ecliptic obliquity, making the fibration genuinely non-trivial. The transition functions between fibers encode how house boundaries warp with changing Ascendant -- exactly the categorical data of a Grothendieck fibration.

### Doering-Isham Topos Approach to Quantum Mechanics

Doering and Isham (2007, arXiv:0803.0417) proposed that physical theories are representations of formal languages in toposes:
- Classical physics uses the topos **Set**.
- Quantum mechanics uses a different topos built from the **poset of commutative subalgebras** of a C*-algebra (the Bohr topos).
- Physical quantities become arrows between a "state-object" and a "quantity-value object" internal to the topos.
- The process of **daseinisation** converts quantum projection operators into subobjects of the spectral presheaf.
- This approach replaces Boolean logic with intuitionistic logic, naturally capturing quantum contextuality.

The **Bohr topos** construction: given a C*-algebra A, form C(A) = poset of commutative subalgebras. The presheaf topos [C(A), Set] is the Bohr topos. Within it, A internalizes as a commutative algebra (Gelfand duality internal to the topos). Gleason's theorem and the Kochen-Specker theorem find natural topos-theoretic formulations.

## 6. Synthesis: The Chain 2-3-7-12-36-72-360 as a Categorical Object

The chain can be understood as a **diagram in the category of finite groups and group homomorphisms**:

```
Z/2Z -> Z/3Z -> Z/7Z -> Z/12Z -> Z/36Z -> Z/72Z -> Z/360Z
```

where each arrow is the canonical inclusion (embedding the smaller cyclic group into the larger one via multiplication by the appropriate quotient). But this is only the surface.

**The deeper structure:**

1. **2 and 3 generate the modular group.** PSL(2,Z) = Z/2Z * Z/3Z. This is the gateway to modular forms, elliptic curves, and the Moonshine connection.

The number 7 is to the chain what the octonions are to the division algebras: the non-associative, exceptional member that breaks regularity and opens the door to deeper structure.
